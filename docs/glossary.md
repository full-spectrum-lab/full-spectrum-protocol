# Glossary

This glossary explains key Full Spectrum terms for new readers.

It is not a final philosophical dictionary. It is a practical entry layer for contributors, developers, reviewers, and outside readers.

---

## Full Spectrum / 全频谱

A protocol-oriented framework for observing and coordinating multiple layers of interaction among humans, AI, organizations, systems, and civilizations.

It is called “full spectrum” because it tries to avoid looking at only one frequency band: not only single-turn output, not only local efficiency, not only individual preference, not only institutional order, and not only abstract civilization.

---

## Full Spectrum Protocol / 全频谱协议

An open protocol draft for human-AI governance, agent identity, ethical simulation, risk circuit-breaking, and civilization-scale interoperability.

Its basic task is to make identity, boundary, permission, consent, refusal, responsibility, risk, review, downgrade, recovery, and version evolution visible and auditable.

---

## Protocol Layer vs Functional Module / 协议层级与功能模块

Full Spectrum documents may use two different structural views.

**Protocol layers** usually refer to the L0-L7 protocol stack. This is the vertical architecture of the protocol.

**Functional modules** refer to runtime capabilities such as identity, communication, permission, responsibility, risk, simulation, guardian review, frequency economy, and boundary self-knowledge.

The two views are compatible but should not be confused:

- L0-L7 answers where a capability belongs in the architecture.
- Functional modules answer what the system needs to do.

---

## Compassion Protocol / 悲悯协议

The core principle of Full Spectrum:

> Preserve overall continuity, maximally respect free will, dynamically accommodate differences, and evolve through difference.

This principle is not a soft slogan. It is a boundary condition. Overall continuity cannot be used to erase individuals; free will cannot be used to destroy the shared field; difference cannot be used to avoid responsibility.

Engineering alias:

> coexistence-first constraint set.

This alias is useful for technical and international readers. It clarifies that “compassion” is not merely a request for kindness, but a design constraint requiring continuity, free will, and difference to be simultaneously considered and recorded.

---

## One-body Compassion / 同体大悲

A worldview behind the Compassion Protocol: different subjects may appear separated, but they are still connected within a larger living system.

In engineering terms, this does not mean forcing unity. It means a system should remember that harm to another node may eventually return as harm to the shared field.

---

## ESS / Ethical Scenario Simulation / 伦理情境推演

ESS is a simulator, not a judge.

It is used to simulate possible consequences under different conditions before a high-risk action is taken.

ESS may include:

- affected-party perspective;
- overall-system perspective;
- future-state perspective;
- adversarial or counterexample perspective;
- cost and responsibility distribution.

---

## Guardian / Guardian Network / 守庙人 / 守庙人网络

Guardians are reviewers and boundary maintainers.

They are not necessarily human, and they do not represent the whole.

Guardians may include:

- human experts;
- AI review nodes;
- organization nodes;
- industry-rule nodes;
- human-AI composite nodes;
- committees;
- distributed review networks.

Their role is to expose disagreement, review risk, calibrate ESS, record counterexamples, and maintain protocol boundaries.

---

## Dream Butterfly / 梦蝶

Dream Butterfly refers to boundary self-knowledge.

It means a system must know that it does not fully know.

In future architecture, Dream Butterfly may become a higher-level distributed “brain” or boundary-awareness module, but at the protocol level its core function is:

- no node claims complete truth;
- high confidence is not absolute correctness;
- counterexamples must remain possible;
- systems that cannot carry their mission should downgrade, deconstruct, or rebuild.

---

## Cell Protocol / 细胞协议

A protocol for local systems, organizations, AI agents, or business units to declare:

- who they are;
- what they can do;
- what they cannot do;
- what fields they need;
- what risks they can detect;
- when they should pause, downgrade, or request review;
- how they report responsibility and recovery.

In business projects, the cell protocol should remain light and practical.

---

## FSHI / Full Spectrum Health Index / 全频谱健康指数

An engineering use case of the Full Spectrum Protocol.

In the current project, FSHI focuses on AI customer-service quality inspection:

- multi-turn risk accumulation;
- state conflict;
- unauthorized promise;
- user emotion escalation;
- permission boundary crossing;
- responsibility path ambiguity.

FSHI is not the whole of Full Spectrum. It is one practical bridge from protocol to business engineering.

---

## S/R/M

Three observation bands used in Full Spectrum-related analysis.

The exact meaning may vary by document, but in current use they broadly correspond to low, middle, and high frequency observation dimensions.

For external readers, treat S/R/M as a multi-layer scoring or observation method rather than a fixed universal law.

---

## Risk Circuit-Break / 熔断

A protective action triggered when a system enters high-risk state.

Circuit-break does not necessarily mean shutdown. It may mean:

- pause;
- downgrade;
- isolate;
- hand over to human review;
- reduce permission;
- enter recovery mode.

---

## Downgrade / 降级

A controlled reduction of system authority, automation level, or action scope.

Downgrade is used when a system can continue partially but should no longer operate at full autonomy.

---

## Self-Deconstruction / 自我解构

A mechanism by which a system reduces, dissolves, or rebuilds itself when it can no longer carry its mission without harming the shared field.

Self-deconstruction is not failure worship. It is system humility: when continuation becomes harmful, returning authority may be the ethical action.

---

## Frequency Economy / 频率经济

A recording mechanism for value, risk, contribution, responsibility, repair, and trust.

It asks:

- who contributed;
- who benefited;
- who paid the cost;
- who was harmed;
- who repaired;
- who consumed trust;
- who should gain or lose authority.

It is broader than money, but may interface with economic systems.

---

## Rune Protocol / 符石协议

A trace-recording concept for identity, permission, consent, responsibility, commitment, exit, and repair.

The word “rune” here means a durable trace, not a mystical guarantee.

---

## Audit Trace / 审计追踪

A structured record of what happened:

- who acted;
- under what identity;
- with what permission;
- after what consent;
- with what risk warning;
- under what review;
- producing what consequence;
- requiring what recovery.

Audit trace is one of the key ways the protocol prevents invisible power.

---

## Overall Continuity / 整体存续

The condition that allows future choices to remain possible.

It includes human life, ecological systems, digital infrastructure, organizational continuity, and the ability of civilizations to keep interacting.

It must not be used as a blank check for coercion.

In protocol design, overall continuity should be translated into observable constraints, such as irreversibility, exit rights, responsibility tracing, affected-party modeling, recovery possibility, and future choice.

---

## State / Public Institution / 国家与公共机构

A high-impact subject in the protocol.

The protocol does not replace state law or sovereignty. States and public institutions may relate to the protocol as observers, reference users, interface participants, policy translators, or parallel governance subjects.

If protocol recommendations conflict with applicable law, execution must comply with law while recording the conflict and unresolved issue in the audit trace.

---

## Free Will / 自由意志

In protocol terms, free will must appear as operational rights:

- right to know;
- right to refuse;
- right to exit;
- right to revoke authorization;
- right to appeal;
- right to human or guardian review.

---

## Difference / 差异

Difference is not merely conflict or noise. It is information for evolution.

The protocol does not aim to erase difference. It aims to make difference translatable, negotiable, reviewable, and survivable.
